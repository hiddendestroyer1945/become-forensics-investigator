#   Become Forensics Investigator
*   **Description**: To explain about the, Who are digital forensics investigator basically, digital forensics investigator, roles and responsibilities of digital forensics investigator and steps to become digital forensics investigator.
   
*   **Who are Forensics Investigator**: Digital forensic investigators are those who find evidence of attacks and prepare it in a form that can be presented before the law, and who find digital evidence of crimes against the law and prepare it in a form that can be presented before the law.

*   **DigitalForensics Investigator**: Digital forensic investigators are responsible for finding out how cybercrimes are committed, what type of data was stolen, who is responsible for it, and for retrieving necessary digital evidence from digital devices involved in crimes against the law.
*   **Roles and Responsibilities of DigitalForensics Investigator**: The duties and responsibilities of digital forensic investigators are explained here.
    *   Digital forensic investigators understand the attack, find the source of the attack, and recover data.
    *   Digital forensic investigators collect and preserve evidence of the attack.
    *   Digital forensic investigators recover or reconstruct data from damaged hard drives and other storage devices. But if data is lost Digital forensic investigators recover data from hard drives and other storage devices. 
    *   Digital forensic investigators collect data using forensic software programs and analyze the collected data.
    *   Digital forensic investigators find security vulnerabilities in computers and networks.
    *   Digital forensic investigators are assess the future and scope of the attack.
    *   Digital forensic investigators provide expert investigative documentation regarding digital evidence found.
    *   Digital forensic investigators prepare and review expert investigation reports on the digital evidence found.
    *   Digital forensic investigators report detected cyberattacks, their evidence, and investigation reports to law enforcement.
